# Stimulus-Response Relationship
Defining the relationship between a stimulus and a response is difficult.
- Neural responses are complex.
- Neural responses are variable.

Neurons respond to a stimulus by producing complex sequences of spikes. The spike sequence reflects...
1) Dynamics of the neuron.
2) Temporal characteristics of the stimulus.

Neural responses can vary across trials despite the same stimulus being presented in each trial.

Many potential sources of variability exist during a trial of firing.
- Variable levels of arousal and attention.
- Randomness associated with biophysical processes that impact neuronal firing.
- Other cognitive processes taking place

Many neurons respond to a given stimulus. Stimulus features are therefore encoded by the activities of large neuron populations.
- Studying population coding requires examining...
	1) Studying firing patterns of individual neurons in the population.
	2) Studying the relationship of firing patterns between the neurons which respond to a stimulus.
