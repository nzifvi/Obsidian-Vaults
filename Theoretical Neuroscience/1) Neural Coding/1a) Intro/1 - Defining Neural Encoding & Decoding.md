# What is Neural Coding?
Neurons propagate signals rapidly, over large distances. This is done by generating electrical pulses called action potentials (APs).
- APs aka spikes.

Neurons represent, and transmit, information by firing sequences of spikes temporally.
- This means that neurons encode information within their spike trains (a sequence of spikes) over time.

Neural coding involves measuring, and defining, how attributes of a stimulus are represented by spikes.
- Stimulus attributes could be light, sound, intensity, etc.
# What is Neural Encoding (NE)?
NE is how to map the stimulus to the spikes generated in response to an observation of said stimulus.

# What is Neural Decoding (ND)?
ND is the inverse of NE. It is how spikes, generated in response to an observed stimulus, are mapped to a stimulus.
- Challenge is to identify attributes of a stimulus based on spikes.
