# Neuron Macro-Anatomy

![[Pasted image 20260611193708.png]]

A neuron consists of 3 main parts:
- Dendrites.
- Axon.
- Soma

An axon is the structure of a neuron which sends action potentials (APs) to other neurons.
- APs generated in the axon.
- Once generated, APs propagate down Axon and result in release of neurotransmitters.

The soma is where the nucleus, which houses the neuron's DNA, is.
- Also where proteins are made and transported throughout neuron's axon and dendrites.

Dendrites are the input structure of neuron.
- Dendrites receive synaptic inputs from axons of other neurons.
- The sum of the APs received through a neuron's dendrites determine if said neuron will fire an AP.

# Action Potentials (APs) and Axons
APs are of great importance.
- They are only form of variable membrane potential which can propagate over large distances.
- APs, unlike other membrane potentials, are regenerated as they propagate through the Axon.

APs terminate at synapses, at the end of axon branches, where the voltage of the AP opens ion channels.
- Opening of ion channel produces an influx of $Ca^{2+}$.
- Opening of ion channels results in release of neurotransmitter.

Neurotransmitters bind to receptors in the synapses in the dendrites of the receiving neuron.
