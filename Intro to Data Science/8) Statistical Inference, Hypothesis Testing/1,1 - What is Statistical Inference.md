# 1) What is Statistical Inference?
Statistical inference begins from drawing conclusions about a population based on a sample of the population.

Statistical inference assumes UNIFORM SAMPLING:
- The probability of picking a data point to be part of a sample should be uniform.
-  Any outcome has a 1/n probability to be part of the sample, where n is the size of the population.
- Experiments should be designed to ensure each outcome has an equal probability to occur.

The goal of statistical inference is to become confident about a statistic of a population being within a given interval.
- Called the confidence interval ([[1,2 - Confidence Intervals]]).
- As more data points added to sample, statistics from sample will change.
- Statistic about a sample isn't the same as a statistic about the population which cannot be calculated.
