# What are Descriptive Statistics?
Descriptive statistics are statements about a given sample produced from a population.

Descriptive statistics cannot tell things about the population the sample was produced from, they can only describe parts of the sample.
- Inferential statistics are used to make statements about the population using a sample.

An example of some descriptive statistics are...
1) Mean
2) Variance.
3) Standard Deviation.
... Others do exist.
