# What are Quantiles?
Quantiles are a type of measure which allow a sample to be divided into equally sized groups which contain an equal number of observations.

Common types of quantiles are:
1) Quartiles.
2) Percentiles.

# Quartiles
The sample is divided into 4 equal parts: Q1 (25th percentile), Q2 (50th percentile, aka median). and Q3 (75th percentile).

# Percentiles
Percentiles are a statistical measuring % of observations in a dataset that fall below a particular percentile.
- E.g: 75th percentile = 75% of outcomes in a sample will be beneath

Percentiles can be used to rank outcomes in a sample. If an outcome is in the n$^{th}$ percentile, it is greater than n% of the outcomes of the sample.
- Values greater than percentile will be above percentile.
- Values less than percentile will be below a given percentile.

To calculate a percentile of a sample S
$$
PL = \frac{p\cdot(|S|+1)}{100}
$$
- PL = desired percentile location.
- p is the desired percentile.
- $|S|$ is the size of the sample.
