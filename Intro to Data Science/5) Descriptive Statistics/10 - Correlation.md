# What is Correlation?
Correlation is a standardised measure of the strength, and direction, of a linear relationship between 2 random variables.
- Derived from covariance.
- Covariance only indicates direction of a linear relationship. Correlation indicates BOTH strength and direction.
$$
Corr(X,Y)
$$
$$
Cor(X,Y)=\frac{Cov(X,Y)}{\sqrt{VAR(X)\cdot VAR(Y)}}
$$

If...
- $Corr(X,Y) > 0$, positive correlation, as one random variable increases, the other tends to as well.
- $Corr(X,Y) < 0$, negative correlation, as one random variable decreases, the other tends to as well.
- $Corr(X,Y) = 0$, no correlation, there is no linear relationship between the 2 random variables.
