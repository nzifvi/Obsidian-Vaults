# Population
The population is the set of all possible outcomes.
- Unfeasible to study the entire population: too large, too costly.
- Sample of the population taken instead.


# Sample
A sample is a subset of the population of all possible outcomes.
- Easier to measure.
- Representability issues: a sample may not be representative of the population.
	- Need to ensure the sample is large and diverse enough.
