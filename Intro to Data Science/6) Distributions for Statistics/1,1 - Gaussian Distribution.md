# What is the Gaussian Distribution
Gaussian distribution of a continuous random variable, $X$, denoted as $X~N(E[X], \sigma_{X}^{2})$
- Expected value, $E[X]$, may also be expressed as $\mu_X$
- Since a Gaussian distribution requires a continuous random variable, $f_{X}(x)$ will be a PDF.

A Gaussian distribution $N$, is a continuous probability distribution of a sample which is defined by 2 parameters.
- Let $X$ be a random variable of a sample.
- Gaussian distribution's probability density function (PDF) is equal to...

$$
f_{X}(x)=\frac{1}{\sigma_{X}\cdot\sqrt{2\pi}}e^-{\frac{(x-\bar{x})^2}{2\sigma_{X}^{2}}}
$$
The two parameters are:
1) $\sigma_X$ 
	- $\sigma_X$ is the standard deviation of sample the random variable $X$ denotes.

2) $\bar{x}$
	- $\bar{x}$ is the mean of the sample the random variable $X$ denotes.



# Interpreting a Gaussian Distribution
1) A Gaussian distribution is symmetrical about the expected value, $E[X]$, of a random variable of a sample $X$

2) The standard deviation of a sample, $\sigma$, determines the "width" of the Gaussian distribution.
	- As $\sigma$ -> $\infty$, the "width" of the Gaussian distribution increases.
	- As $\sigma$ -> 0, the "width" of the Gaussian distribution decreases.
