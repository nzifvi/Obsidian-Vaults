## What are Random Variables?
Random variables are a set which models an unknown, abstract function, that maps the outcomes of an experiment to given values.

- $X(\omega)$ denotes the random variable X over the set of possible outcomes from an experiment $\omega$
- Random variable $X(\omega)$ can take on any 1 possible outcome from $\omega$

$$
P[\{ \omega \in \Omega | X(\omega) = 4 \}]
$$
- $\omega_1$ outcome is a member of the sample space $\Omega$
- Short hand version exists...
$$
P[\{ \omega \in \Omega | X(\omega) = 4 \}] = P[X = 4]
$$

## Support of Random Variables
Support of a random variables is a description of the possible values a random variable can be.
- Describes a domain.

$$
X(\omega) = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}
$$
$$
Supp(X(\omega)) = \{0,8\}
$$

The support of a random variable can be...
1) Discrete.
	- Outcomes are countable and do not have any other possible values than the ones described in the set.

2) Continuous.
	- Can be ANY value between the given bounds. 
	- Continuous random variables are infinite since there is always a number between 2.
$$
X(\omega) = [0,1]
$$
$$
X(\omega)=0.1
$$
$$
X(\omega)=0.01
$$
$$
X(\omega)=0.001
$$
$$
X(\omega)=0.0........1
$$
