# 1) Supervised Learning
In supervised learning, each element of the training data has labels associated with it.
- Labels are the "output" of the element.
- E.g: for binary classification, each element of the training data could be labelled if the patient is or is not at risk of heart disease.

From the training data, a model should be able to generalise it.
- Be good at predicting the output for unseen data.
