# What does a Psychology Experiment Look Like?
1) Participants complete some sort of task. For example, could be...
	- Memorising something.
	- Recalling a list of words.
	- etc.

2) Task should require a cognitive ability under study.

3) Human behaviour is noisy so task is repeated many times (many trials)
	- More trials = more noise is cancelled out.

4) People vary in abilities, motivation, behaviour, and previous experience.
	- To increase reliability of experiment, data should be collected from multiple people.
	- Sometimes, an experiment may study something with doesn't vary a lot between people, therefore less people can be used for the experiment.

5) Participants do not always do what is wanted of them
	- Must control participant expectations and demand characteristics.
	- Demand characteristics: cues which inform the participant of the study's purpose and allow them to act accordingly.

# RCTs
RCT = randomised control trial
- Type of trial used for medical studies
- RCTs are one of the best models of experiment to use, due to eliminating multiple issues.

![[Pasted image 20260129114608.png]]

RCTs have 2 groups: control and test.
- Control group are not tested on.
- Test group are tested on.

Outcomes of participants are measured via an outcome.
- Outcomes between groups are compared to see if a difference exists.

RCTs are...
1)  Double blind:
	- Participants nor researchers know who is in which group (control or test group)
2) Randomised:
	- Participants of study are randomly put into either group. No group allocation bias exists this way.
	- Prevents situations where a group has confounding variables.
