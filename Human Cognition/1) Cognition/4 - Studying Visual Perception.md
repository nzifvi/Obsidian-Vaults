# How to Study Visual Perception
Visual perception is studied by...
- Controlled experiments
- Manipulating stimulus features and observing observable changes in behaviour or brain activity.

To test visual perception, experiments should be designed which test specific hypotheses about how the system works.

# Examples of Perception Studies
## 1) Motion Perception Experiment
To study motion perception, random dot kinematograms (RDKs) are used.
- A cloud of noisily moving dots.

![[Pasted image 20260129120348.png]]

Participants report whether motion is moving in one direction or another.

The task's difficulty should be varied.
- Increase noise of motion to make it less difficult, as motion is more observable.
- Decrease noise of motion to make it more difficult, as motion is less observable.

RDKs are one of the simplest motion stimuli.
- Like a simplified toy model of motion in the real world.

Results of RDK usually follows a pattern.
- As coherence of dot's motion decreases, accuracy gets worse.
- Eventually approaches a point where participants have no idea of the answer.
## 2) Perception Speed Test
To test the speed of perception, a visual flicker can be used.
- Visual flickers are a shape which flashes different colours at a given rate of time/frequency.
- Colours, and the number of colours shown, can be customised.
- Different shapes can be used.

![[Pasted image 20260129121133.png]]

2 ways methods to control visual flicker tests:
1) Visual Flicker Fusion Thresholds
2) Method of Constant Stimuli

### 2.1) Visual Flicker Fusion Thresholds
1) Frequency of alterations between colours are manipulated.
2) Collect ratings on if there are 1 or 2 colours.
3) Look at the frequency where only one colour is reported.

By finding frequency where only one colour is reported, the temporal resolution (speed) of perception for these stimuli are found.
- Test is the reason why most TVs have a refresh rate of approx. 60Hz.

### 2.2) Method of Constant Stimuli
1) Manipulate frequency of alterations between colours.
	- Vary flicker speed and contrast.
2) Show participants two videos: one with flickering circles, one with a 120Hz flicker.
	- 120Hz appears to be constant.
3) Participants report which video has the flickering circles.
4) Look for the frequency where 80% of participants answer the video which has alterations != 120Hz has the flickering circles.
## 3) Reaction Speed Test
Reaction speed tests evaluate how fast choices, or reactions, are made.
- Tested by measuring how fast a response occurs given a stimuli.
- Some of response speed relates to motor execution, some is the process of deciding how to respond.
- Change aspects of the task/stimuli to observe how decision speed changes.

Response speed is easy to observe.
