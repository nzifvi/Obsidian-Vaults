# 1) Approaches to Studying Cognition
Approaches to study cognition are:
1) Introspectionism
2) Behaviourism
3) Cognitive & Computational Psychology

## 1.1) Introspectionism
Cognitive processes are studied via self-reflection and self observation.

## 1.2) Behaviourism
Focuses on measuring observed behaviour.
- Via lens of Behaviourism, behaviour is a function of learned experiences and stimuli.
- Behaviour is a learned response to a given stimuli.

Behaviourism resulted in methods such as classical and operant conditioning.
- Leading to reinforcement learning.

## 1.3) Cognitive & Computational Psychology
Uses scientific empiricism of Behaviourism to study the mind and cognition.

Computational models help formulate theories of how tasks are solved by mind.
- An algorithm, code, equation that map inputs to outputs can be formulated.
- That model can then be tested by comparing with observed behaviour.


# 2) How is Cognition Studied?
Given a task, questions like the ones below, are asked:
- What information is being used to solve a task
- What abilities are required to solve a task.
- How fast can the task be completed and what determines the speed.
