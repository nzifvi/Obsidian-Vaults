# What is Cognition?
Cognition = mental act or process by which knowledge is acquired. Cognition includes...
- Perception.
- Intuition.
- Reasoning

Cognition is also the knowledge that results from the act/processes mentioned above.

Brain doesn't function in isolation therefore cognition doesn't either.
- Cognition has 4 Es of cognition theory:
	1) Embodied
		- We have a body.
	2) Embedded
		- Our body exists in a physical environment which applies constraints to what actions can be done.
	3) Extended
		- Cognition occurs through objects in the physical world: notebooks, diaries, communication.
	4) Enactive
		- Cognitive processes evolve over time and space.

# Cognition and AI
Cognitive science takes inspiration from human intelligence and neuronal structure & function, applying them to build AI systems which perform tasks well.

AI allows for theories of cognition to be tested.

# Why Cognition?
- Building intelligent systems requires an understanding of (human) intelligence.
- Testing for AI requires understanding how to test for cognitive abilities.
- To build human-like systems, human cognition must be understood.
