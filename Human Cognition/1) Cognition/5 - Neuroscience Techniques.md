# Neuroscience Terminology
#### Temporal Resolution
Temporal resolution = the ability for a technique to demonstrate when neuronal activation has occurred. 
#### Spatial Resolution
Spatial resolution = the ability for a technique to demonstrate which brain region is active
# Non-Behavioural Techniques
## 1) Electrophysiology & Single Neuron Recording
Measures voltage of a single neuron (both temporal and spatial summation).
![[Pasted image 20260129122417.png]]
- Highly invasive: a voltmeter must be put into the body to probe a neuron.

Single Neuron Recordings can answer...
- What are these neurons responsive to.
- What is the firing rate of these neurons.
## 2) Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI)
fMRI is useful to concentrate on, or compare different, regions of the brain.
- Has high spatial resolution.
- Has low temporal resolution.
![[Pasted image 20260129122643.png]]

fMRIs can answer...
- Which brain regions are involved when handling process X.
- Where is information X represented.
- Is the information in task X like the information in task Y (question known as decoding)
## 3)Electroencephalography (EEG)
EEGs are good for concentrating on time courses of processes.
- Have high temporal resolution.
- Have low spatial resolution.

![[Pasted image 20260129122806.png]]

EEGs can answer...
- How much information is communicated across regions of the brain.
- How much faster is this kind of stimulus processed.
- Is the information in task X like that in task Y (question known as decoding)
