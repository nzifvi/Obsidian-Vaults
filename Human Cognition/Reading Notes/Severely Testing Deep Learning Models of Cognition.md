# Deep Neural Networks and their Problems
Deep Neural Networks, DNNs, are increasingly used for modelling in neuroscience field.
- DNNs suffer from conceptual & methodological problems which result in unsupported conclusions and claims about cognitive processes. 

Problems include:
- Logical fallacies.
- Overclaiming.
- Unchecked degrees of freedom.
- Naive empiricism.
- Measurement and interpretation mismatch.
- Inadequate theories.

Overall issue: Similarities between DNNs and human brains are not as similar as believed.

The claim that DNNs are similar to the human brain doesn't stand up when it is severely tested.
# What is Severe Testing?
Severe testing is a concept regarding how strongly a statistical test, used for hypothesis testing, actually probes a given hypothesis.

A result ONLY supports a hypothesis if the statistical test is capable of finding issues with the hypothesis.
- A result of a statistical test isn't enough alone to confirm a hypothesis as...
	1) Sample used might not be truly representative or large enough.
	2) Statistical test may have been conducted incorrectly.
	3) Many other reasons.

Severe testing probes...
1) How much of an attempt was made to falsify the hypothesis.
2) If the hypothesis was wrong, would the statistical test have detected a discrepancy.

## Weak Severity Requirement
Weak severity requirement is the most minimal condition that is part of severe testing .
> Data provides a hypothesis ONLY if the hypothesis passes a test that it would have a non-negligible chance of failing if the hypothesis were false.

Mandatory parts of weak severity requirement:
- The used statistical test MUST be capable of, or have a way to, detect some relevant errors.
- Passing the statistical test must not be easy or a guaranteed.

The weak severity requirement prevents obviously bad tests from being used in hypothesis tests.
- Does not guarantee strong or reliable evidence.

The weak severity exists to prevent evidence from:
- Tiny samples.
- Extremely noisy measurements.
- Tests with near-zero power.
- Vague hypotheses that are almost impossible to falsify.
