# What are Perceptual Decisions
A perceptual decision is a cognitive process that converts noisy, ambiguous sensory inputs into discrete, categorical judgements.
- These categorical judgements guide behaviour.

Multiple perceptual functions exist when processing the sensory input data. Perceptual functions refine sensory input data. 
- Signal detection.
- Spatial localisation.
- Discrimination.

Sometimes, a perceptual decision goes beyond perceptual functions and requires higher order functions.
- Things like "what are contents of speech", "what is meaning of speech" are higher order functions

ALL perceptual functions interact to build a perceptual decision
- When studying perception, only one of these perceptual functions are focused on.
- However these perceptual functions do not exist in isolation.

Perceptual experience is constructed from MANY smaller perceptual decisions.

## Psychophysics
Psychophysics is the study of perception.
- Studies relationship between stimuli intensity and sensation and perception.
- Manipulate one, or more, controlled variables then measure the effects on behaviour of participant.
