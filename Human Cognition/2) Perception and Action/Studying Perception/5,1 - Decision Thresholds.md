# Decision Thresholds
Decision thresholds are a fundamental component of a decision making process.
- Often have the greatest ability to explain something in a test.

Certain parameters of a visual stimuli that are under study require a threshold to be specified to differentiate between n states.
- Important for discrimination tasks

![[Pasted image 20260207155225.png]]

MUST determine a threshold at which answers are true if above and false is below (or vice versa, depending on study)

Decision thresholds separate an evidence space into partitions.
- n thresholds can be defined, creating n partitions.

Decision thresholds represent how you...
- Label.
- Interpret.
- Classify.
... a piece of information


Let $\mathcal{O}$ denote the decision threshold.
- For an answer x, given by a participant.

Classification could look like...
$$
result(x) = 
\begin{cases}
x < \mathcal{O} \Rightarrow result(x) = result_1
\\
x = \mathcal{O} \Rightarrow result(x) = result_2
\\
x > \mathcal{O} \Rightarrow result(x) = result_3
\end{cases}
$$
