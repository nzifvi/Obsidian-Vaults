# Psychophysical Tasks
Psychophysical tasks are tasks research participants are asked to perform to test a perceptual function in as much isolation as possible.

Listed below are 4. These 4 are not the only ones

Stimuli should be controlled tightly and manipulated.
- The effect of changing the stimuli on the research participant should be observed.
- To control for response biases if asking a question, very specific questions must be asked.
# Detection
Detection tasks focus on if a stimulus, such as a flash of light, was present or absent
# Discrimination
Discrimination tasks focus on whether the stimulus was of type A or B.
- Was the stimulus tilting towards the left or right.
# Same/Different
Same/Different tasks focus on whether 2 given stimuli were the same or not.

# Adjustment Tasks
Adjustment tasks involve research participants adjusting a parameter of the control variable until it looks right to them.
