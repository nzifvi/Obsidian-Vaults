# What are V1 Neurons
V1 Neurons are located in the primary visual cortex.
- V1 neurons are first stage of processing visual information.

![[Pasted image 20260224110701.png]]

Two types of V1 neurons: simple and complex.

## V1 Simple Cells
V1 simple cells are responsible for detecting specific, low-level features in V1 simple cell's receptive field.

V1 simple cells detect...
- Orientated edges.
- Bars.
- Gratings

# V1 Neuron Cells detecting Edges
Each individual V1 neuron has a preferred orientation.
- Fires more when an edge in receptive field has a preferred rotation.

![[Pasted image 20260224111224.png]]

As bar's orientation approaches the preferred orientation of a given V1 neuron, said neuron will begin to fire more.
- Fires less, sometimes not at all, when bar is not in V1 neuron's preferred orientation.

Primary Visual Cortex has many V1 neurons, all with their own preferred orientation.
