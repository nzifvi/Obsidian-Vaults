# What is Active Inference?
Active inference is a hierarchical Bayesian inference model of cognition and action.
- First proposed to be a model of predictive coding.
- Now, Active Inference is a model of brain function, not neural function.

Basic idea of active inference:
- Brain is minimising long term uncertainty.
- The brain achieves this by...
	1) Learning.
	2) Making itself enter predicted states.

![[Pasted image 20260305133400.png]]

Purpose of perception is to predict the world states.

Purpose of action is to test perceptual predictions and update them with sensory feedback.

Active inference = powerful framework. High-level prediction are goals.
>"I will get a first" is modelled as P($1^{st}$ ) = "High"

Attention is approximately modelled as a weight of prediction errors.
# Features of Active Inference
1) Top-down control of precision and attention

2) High precision beliefs at high levels. Belief states are measured as goal states.

3) Gives a decision-based framework to cast cognition in.

# Problems with Active Inference
Active inference is...
1) Untestable.
	- Cannot be scientifically falsified.
2) Too flexible.
3) Intractable.
4) Has been overstretched and applied to everything
