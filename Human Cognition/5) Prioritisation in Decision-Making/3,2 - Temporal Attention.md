# What is Temporal Attention?
Temporal attention is used to pay attention to particular points in time.
- Thought of as anticipation.

Temporal attention prioritises sensory processing at the attended points in time.
- Results are faster during attended points in time.
- Responses are more accurate during attended points in time.

Before the expected point in time...
- Neural resources ramp up in advance of expected point in time.
- Sensory systems are pre-tuned before stimulus arrives.
- Processing is deeper and faster at attended moments in time.

Temporal attention can be manipulated by cyclically repeating a stimulus: causing the brain to...
1) Extract the temporal structure.
2) Model it with an oscillator.
	- Peaks of oscillator corresponds to expected moments in time.
3) Align peaks of neuron excitability with the peaks of the oscillator.
