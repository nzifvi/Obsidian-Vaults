# Spatial Attention
Spatial attention is used to attend to regions of space.
- Acts as a spotlight of attention.

Spatial attention can be either
1) Exogenous if a stimulus provokes it.
2) Endogenous if consciously paying attention to location in space.

Spatial attention measurable by looking at response speeds in exogenous or endogenous Posner Cueing paradigms.

Spatial attention speed responses:
- Exogenous spatial attention increases detectability and perceptual performance (measured by SDT's dprime)
- Endogenous spatial attention may improve perceptual performance and/or bias perception.
