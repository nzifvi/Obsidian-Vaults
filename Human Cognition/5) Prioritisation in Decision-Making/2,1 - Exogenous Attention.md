# What is Exogenous Attention?
Exogeneous (from outside) attention is attention which is driven by a stimulus occurring external to the body.
- Aka attentional capture.
	- Called attentional capture as exogeneous attention is involuntarily captured by stimulus in environment.
- Aka bottom-up capture.

Exogeneous attention is REFLEXIVE.
- Not under executive control. Happens as a reflex.
- Exogeneous attention operates automatically without conscious intent.

Exogeneous attention is driven by stimulus properties such as:
1) Intensity (a loud noise).
2) Predictability (surprising stimuli)
	- A stimuli which is unexpected is flagged by nervous system as high-priority. Exogeneous attention is captured.
3) Smoothness of capture:
	- A stimuli which appears suddenly captures exogeneous attention.

Perception is facilitated using exogeneous attention.
