# What is Synaptic Plasticity?
Synaptic Plasticity is the method which enables the brain to retain information.
- Synaptic strength can be changed rapidly.

Synaptic plasticity can change due to changes to pre-or-post synaptic cell function.
- Changing amount of presynaptic calcium present.
- Changing response of postsynaptic receptor(s) to transmitter.

# Tetanic Stimulation
Tetanic stimulation is when a neuron, or synapse, is stimulated by rapid, high frequency bursts of electric stimulation.
- Causes neuron to fire repeatedly in quick succession. 

![[Pasted image 20260309214641.png]]

Tetanic stimulation of synapse causes large influx of calcium ions in postsynaptic neuron.
- Triggers cascade of biological changes.

# Potentiation
Potentiation is an increase in synaptic strength.
- Postsynaptic response becomes stronger/larger.

Two types of potentiation exist:
1) Post-tetanic potential (PTP)
2) Long-term potentiation (LTP)

## Post-tetanic Potentiation
Short-term increase in synaptic strength that occurs immediately after tetanic stimulation.
- Only lasts for seconds to minutes.
- Happens due to residual calcium ions left in presynaptic terminal which cause more neurotransmitters to be released.
- Fades as residual calcium is cleared in presynaptic terminal.

## Long-term Potentiation 
Long-term potentiation, LTP, is a long-lasting increase in synaptic strength that occurs after tetanic stimulation.
- LTP is the cellular basis of learning and memory.

Hebb's Rule:
> Neurons that fire together, wire together.

LTP works through several mechanisms.
- Burst of stimulation activates AMPA receptors: depolarising postsynaptic membrane.
- Depolarisation removes magnesium ion block from NMDA receptors.
- NMDA receptors open: allowing calcium ions to flood into postsynaptic cell.
- Calcium ions activates enzymes insert more AMPA receptors into synapse.
- Over time, structural changes occur and the synapse physically grows stronger and more sensitive.
