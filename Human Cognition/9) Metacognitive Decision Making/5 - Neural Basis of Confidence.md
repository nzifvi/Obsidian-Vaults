# Neural Basis of Confidence
Neural process of providing confidence judgements involve multiple parts of the brain working together.

The anterior prefrontal cortex (aPFC) is the primary region involved in the process of SPECIFICALLY generating confidence reports.
- demonstrated by Fleming et al.

Key evidence of the aPFC being a key region involved in generating confidence reports is:
1) fMRI studies
	- When participants rate confidence vs perform a control task, aPFC shows selective action (activated more during rating of confidence versus a control task)
2) Lesion studies (fleming et al., 2014)
	- aPFC lesions resulted in impaired perceptual metacognitive monitoring.
	- A control group and aPFC lesioned group performed equally well in a task and were equally confident.
	- However, aPFC had worse perceptual metacognitive monitoring.
