# 2 Levels of Decision Making
Two forms of decision making exist: objective and metacognitive.

## 1) Objective Decisions (Type 1)
With objective decisions, the object of decision is external world stimuli.
- Objective decisions consist of decisions made about an external stimuli.

Examples of objective decisions are:
- "Is the stimulus left of right"
- "Is this a face or a house"
- "Is the motion upward?"

Objective decisions are measured by d' from SDT.

## 2) Metacognitive Decisions (Type 2)
With metacognitive decisions, the object of decision are cognitive states.
- Metacognitive decisions consist of decisions about your own thoughts/decisions.

Examples of metacognitive decisions are:
- "Was I correct or incorrect about decision X"
- "How confident am I about X"

Metacognitive decisions are measured by meta d' from SDT.

# 2 Forms of Metacognition
Two forms of metacognition exist.

The types of decisions feed into specific types of metacognition.
- Objective decisions, type 1, act as inputs for metacognitive monitoring.
- Metacognitive decisions, type 2, act as inputs for metacognitive control.

## 1) Metacognitive Monitoring
Metacognitive monitoring is the assessing the quality/state of cognition.

It includes...
- Confidence judgements (self measurements of ability/correctness)
- Self-efficacy measurements.
- Feeling of knowing.
## 2) Metacognitive Control
Metacognitive control is the adjusting of cognitive processes based on cognitive monitoring.

It includes...
- Allocating more attention.
- Changing strategy.
- Adjusting decision bias.
- Opting out of hard trials.
