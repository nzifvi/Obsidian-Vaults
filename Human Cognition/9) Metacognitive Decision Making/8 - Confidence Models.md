# Modelling Confidence
Confidence can be modelled via 2 ways:
1) Evidence accumulation models (DDM/SDT).
2) Bayesian models.
3) Heuristic models.

NOTE: all models which model confidence are models of type 2 decisions: metacognitive decisions.
- Models metacognitive monitoring.

# 1) Evidence Accumulation Models: DDM
Evidence accumulation models work similarly to decision-making models.
- Confidence is modelled as the amount of evidence accumulated at time t.

Confidence comes from the SAME evidence accumulation process as choice
- Confidence is also modelled by DDM.

Measured activity in lateral interparietal (LIP) neurons associated with the "correct" choice.
- LIP neuron activity REFLECTS amount of evidence accumulation for both choice and confidence

In DDM, evidence accumulates over time (drift rate) to make a choice.
- Once choice is made, evidence accumulation continues.
- Confidence = amount of evidence accumulated after the choice is made.

# SDT Confidence Model
SDT can be used to model metacognition

Type-1 SDT, about an objective decision, models how well somebody distinguishes signal from noise. Type-2 SDT models how well somebody distinguishes their correct responses from their incorrect ones.
- Type-2 TP = highly confident about decision X and actually correct.
- Type-2 FP = highly confident about decision X but actually incorrect.

Type-2 SDT provides meta-d', rather than just d'
- meta-d' represents how well a confidence rating differences between a participant's correct and incorrect responses.
- Acts like a measure of how accurate self-efficacy is.

The m-ratio represents metacognitive efficiency.
- meta-d' is influenced by how easy or hard a task was.
- Better to interpret using m-ratios.
