# Estimating Confidence without External Feedback
External feedback of actions is often received yet it can be...
1) Delayed.
2) Unreliable.
3) Sparse
Therefore, uncertainty must be estimated in the absence of reliable external feedback.

Confidence judgements are made about...
- External state/content.
	- External state/content confidence judgements consist of "I don't know whether this is state A or B"
- Internal state/decision.
- Task performance.
	- Act as a measure of confidence in how a task has been performed.
- Ability.
	- Acts as a measure of confidence in ability.
