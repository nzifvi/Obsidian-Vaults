# 1) What are Deep Neural Networks (DNNs)
DNNs are neural networks which feature more than one hidden layer.

The more hidden units a shallow neural network has, the more their ability to model functions improves.
- With enough hidden units, shallow neural networks can describe many complex functions.
- However, for some functions, the required no. of hidden units is too large.

DNNs can produce more linear regions than shallow neural networks.
- DNNs can be used to describe more functions.
