# Synonymy
A synonymy is a type of relationship, between n words, which all mean the same thing.
- n words are synonymous if they can be substituted in place of one another, in all possible contexts, without altering the meaning of a sentence.

True synonyms are very rare.

Choice of a given synonym usually gives more information about something in the sentence.

# Antonymy
An antonymy is a type of relationship between words which are opposite in meaning.
- Substituting one for another in a sentence causes the sentence to be negated.

Antonyms are usually similar in meaning.
- Hot and cold, antonyms, both describe the temperature of something.

Most antonyms are pairs of adjectives, verbs, or adverbs.

# Hyponymy & Hypernymy
Hyponymy and Hypernymy are linguistics terms which capture the idea of classes of words and class inclusions..

"A dog is a type of animal"
- Dog is a hyponym of animal.
- Animal is a hypernym of dog.

A hyponym is a word which is more specific than another word.
- Hyponyms are more specific whilst hypernyms are generic terms that refer to an entire class.

Hypernyms refer to an entire set, or class, of words.
- To identify a hypernym. Think if there are multiple things which are defined by that word.
