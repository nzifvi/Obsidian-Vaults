## Standard Deviations of Normal Distribution
The Standard Deviation tells you, on average, how far the possible values x differ from the mean of a Normal Distribution.

The Empirical Rule states that:
1) Within 1 standard deviation ($\mu \pm 1\cdot \sigma$), 68% of possible values of x will be found.
2) Within 2 standard deviations ($\mu \pm 2 \cdot \sigma$), 95% of possible values of x will be found (including values from 1st standard deviation).
3) Within 3 standard deviations ($\mu \pm 3 \cdot \sigma$), 99.7% of possible values of x will be found (including values from 1st and 2nd standard deviations).

![[Pasted image 20250421125900.png]]
From the above Bell Curve...
- $\mu = 3.5$
- $\sigma = 0.8$

Applying the Empirical Rule...
1) 68% of possible values of x will be found within 1 standard deviation of $\mu$
	- $\mu - \sigma = 3.5 - 0.8 = 2.7$
	- $\mu + \sigma = 3.5 + 0.8 = 4.3$
	- 68% of possible values x found within range $x\in [2.7, 4.3]$

![[Pasted image 20250421130342.png]]

2) 95% of possible values of x will be found within 2 standard deviations of $\mu$
	- $\mu - 2\sigma = 3.5 - 2(0.8) = 1.9$
	- $\mu + 2\sigma = 3.5 + 2(0.8) = 5.1$
	- 95% of possible values of x will be found within range $x\in [1.9, 5.1]$
