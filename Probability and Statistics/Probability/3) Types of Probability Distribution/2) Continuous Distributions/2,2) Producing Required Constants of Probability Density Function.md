## 1) Producing Mean $\mu$

Mean of a data set can be produced by...

- Summing all data points part of set.
- Dividing by the number of data points (cardinality of data set).

$$
\mu = \frac{1}{n}\sum^{n}_{i-1}[x_i]
$$

## 2) Producing Variance $\sigma^2$

The mean, $\mu$, is required to produce a data set's variance.

The variance can be produced by...
- Summing the differences of squares of each data point minus the mean: $(x_{i} - \mu)^2$
- Dividing by n, the number of data points (cardinality of data set).

$$
\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{n}\sum^{n}_{i-1}[(x_{i}-\mu)^2]
$$

## 3) Producing Standard Deviation $\sigma$

The Standard Deviation of a data set is the square root of it's variance.

$$
\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2}
$$
