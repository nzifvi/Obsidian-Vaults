## Sample Spaces
Mathematic framework for probability relies on sets as a foundation.

For an experiment performed, the outcome of the experiment is an element of a set of all possible outcomes.
- Before the experiment, it is unknown which outcome of the set of possible outcomes will be the result.
- After the experiment, the possible outcome for that experiment is known.

#### Sample Space
Sample space, S, of an experiment is the set of all possible outcomes of that experiment.

$$S= \{1,2,3,4,5\}$$
A sample space, of an experiment, can be finite, countably infinite, or uncountably infinite.
#### Event
An event, A, is a subset of the Sample space, S.

It is said that the event, A, occurred an experiment's actual outcome is A.

$$A = \{1, 2\}$$
$$A \subset S$$
