## Frequency Probability
Frequentist view of probability is that probability represents a long-run frequency over a large no. of repetitions for any experiment.

E.g: if a coin has a 50% probability to land on heads, the coin will land on heads 50% of the times flipped, n, if the experiment is repeated.

## Bayesian Probability
Bayesian probability is that probability represents a degree of belief about the event, A, in question.

Using Bayesian probability, probabilities can be assigned to hypotheses.

E.g: for a patient in surgery

P(X = live) is the probability that patient will live
P(X = die) is the probability that patient will die

Each P(X) represents a degree of belief about the event.
