## Why study probability?
Probability referred to as many things: luck, coincidence, randomness, uncertainty, risk, doubt, fortune, chance ...

- All above words are probability except these words refer to probability in a vague, casual way.

Probability can be massively counterintuitive, despite it's applications to the world and how many things depend on it.
- If probability is not properly understood, actions made because of predictions, based on incorrect probability, could happen.



Mathematics = logic of certainty. Probability is the logic of uncertainty.

Probability is very useful in a large number of fields, since it provides mechanisms for understanding and explaining variation: separating signals from noise PLUS modelling complex events.
- Probability is applied in the following fields:


#### 1) Statistics
Probability is the foundation of statistics. Probability is what enables statistics the ability to learn things about the world using data.

#### 2) Physics
Fields such as quantum physics depend on probability. Another branch of physics that depends on probability is statistical mechanics.

#### 3) Biology
Genetics heavily relies on probability. Gene inheritance and modelling random mutations rely on probability.

#### 4) Computer Science
Randomised algorithms make decisions whilst they execute. Randomised algorithms are often an important application as they are usually simpler, and more efficient, than currently known deterministic alternatives.

Probability also plays vital part in studying performance of an algorithm.

Probability also plays a vital part in machine learning and AI.

#### 5) Meteorology
Weather forecasts are usually computed and expressed in terms of probability.

#### 6) Gambling
Earliest investigations into probability aimed at answering questions about gambling and games of chance.

#### 7) Finance
Probability is used in quantitative finance. Modelling stock prices over time and determining fair prices for financial instruments are heavily reliant on probability.

#### 8) Political Science
Recently, political science has become increasingly dependent on quantitative and statistical tools.

Statistics is applied through...
- Statistical analysis performed on surveys.
- Predicting probability of election outcomes
- etc.

#### 9) Medicine
Clinical trials make use of probability to ensure medicine, vaccines, or a placebo are truly given to trial participants randomly.

#### 10) Life itself
Life is uncertain and probability is the logic of uncertainty. Probability allows for common fallacies to be avoided, coincidences to be found, and better predictions to be made.
