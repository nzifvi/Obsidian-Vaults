# Sensor Classifications
A sensor can be...
1) Proprioceptive/exteroceptive
2) Passive/active

## Proprioceptive vs. Exteroceptive Sensors

Proprioceptive sensors measure internal system values.
- Motor angular velocity
- Robot arm joint angles.
- Battery voltage.
- etc...

Exteroceptive sensors acquire information about the environment the robot is in.
- Distance measurements.
- Light intensity.
- etc...

# Passive vs. Active Sensors
Passive sensors measure ambient environment energy which enters the sensor.
- Temperature probes.
- Microphones.
- CCDs or CMOS cameras.

Active sensors EMIT energy into the environment and then measure the environmental reaction.

Active sensors often superior to passive sensors.
- Active sensors can manage more controlled interactions w/ environments.
- HOWEVER, active sensors may have interference between it's signal and others beyond it's control
	- Signals emitted by other nearby robots or example.
