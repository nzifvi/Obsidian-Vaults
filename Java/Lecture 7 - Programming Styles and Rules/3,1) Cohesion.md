[[2,1) Coupling]] <- Back

---
- Cohesion refers to the number, and diversity, of tasks and jobs a unit of code is responsible for.
	- Consider classes, and methods, and what their tasks they perform.

- A class, or method, performing a single task is a good thing.
	- It is easier to reuse, adapt, and maintain.

- A class, or method, performing many tasks is a bad thing.
	- Harder to reuse and adapt.

RULE OF THUMB: A class, or method, should have one and only one task.
- The methods of a class work towards accomplishing the task of a class.
- A method will only have a single task.
