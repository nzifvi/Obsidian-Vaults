[[1.2) Default Access Specifier]]

- Access specifiers allow for variables, methods, and inner classes, of a class, to have their visibility declared.
	- Level of visibility determines whether something can be inherited or not.

Three types:
1) public
2) private
3) protected

### public

Any variable, method, or inner class, with a public access specifier will be inheritable by other classes.

Anything with a public access specifier is also accessible OUTSIDE of the class.

### private

Any variable, method, or inner class, with a private access specifier will NOT be inheritable by other classes.

Anything with a private access specifier is NOT accessible OUTSIDE of the class.

### protected

Anything with a protected access specifier cannot be accessed outside of the class, however, they can be inherited.


## Tips:

- Protected should very rarely be used. Proper encapsulation ensures it does not be.
- Methods should be public UNLESS it is only an internally used method.
- Variables should be private.
