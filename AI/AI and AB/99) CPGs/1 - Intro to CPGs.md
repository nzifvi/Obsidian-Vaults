# What are CPGs?
Central Pattern Generators, CPGs, are self-organising biological neural circuits.
- CPGs produce rhythmic outputs from continuous valued inputs.

CPGs are the source of neural activity pattern which control rhythmic motor behaviours like...
- Walking.
- Swimming.
- Breathing.
- Chewing.

To be classed as a rhythmic generator, a CPG must satisfy the below conditions:
- >= 2 processes which interact such that each process sequentially increases and then decreases.
- The system returns to it's starting point repeatedly based on the interaction between >= 2 processes.



# Uses of CPGs
CPGs are involved in locomotion, breathing, rhythm generation, and many auxiliary functions done in the body.

## CPGs controlling Locomotion
Locomotion occurs as a sequence of movements, controlled by the brain stem, rather than a higher order process in the brain.
- Steps in locomotion sequence occurs automatically, rather than as a conscious effort.
