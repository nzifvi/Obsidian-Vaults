# Recap: What is Competing Co-evolution
Competing co-evolution is when n species compete against one another.
- Competition acts as a selective pressure. Species which compete better dominate.

# Red Queen Hypothesis
The Red Queen hypothesis is the idea that a species which competes against other species must constantly adapt, evolve, and reproduce in order to survive.
- If not, species will become extinct.


# Types of Competitive Evolution:
1) Arms Race Competition.
2) Divergent Competition.
3) Exploitation Competition.
4) Interference Competition.
5) Apparent Competition.

## 1) Arms Race Competition
Arms race competition evolutions involve progress, mutual counter-adaptations between species.
- Species A will evolve to counteract species B, species B will then evolve to counteract species A, so on and so on.

Arms race competition evolution are continuous. They escalate over time.

Some examples of arms race competition evolutions are:
1) Predator-prey (cheetah gets faster to catch gazelles -> gazelles get faster to evade cheetahs)
2) Male-male competition.
3) Parasite-host (parasite becomes better at infecting ->  host evolves to have better immune system to fight parasite.)

## 2) Divergent Competition
Divergent competition exists when selection favours avoiding direct competition.
- Resources may be partitioned and shared between "competing" species.
- A species may access a different resource due to the amount of competition for another one (new resource is easier)

Competing species tend to diverge rather than escalate.


## 3) Exploitation Competition 
Exploitation competition exists when the success of a species depends on efficiently using a resource shared with other species.
- Outcome of exploitation competition: better resource harvesting, faster growth, a diet consisting of more things.

## 4) Interference Competition
Interference competition exists when species actively display aggression to exclude competing species.
- The outcome involves evolving to be aggressive, to have the capacity to harm, and display territorial behaviour.

## 5) Apparent Competition
Apparent competition exists when species compete indirectly through shared predators/parasites.
- One species being more successful alters the predator pressure on another species.
- Because there are more of species A there will be more of the predator species which negatively impacts species B.
