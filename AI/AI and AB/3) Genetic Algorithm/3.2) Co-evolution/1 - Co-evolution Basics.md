# 1) What is Co-evolution?
Co-evolution is where n species evolve across multiple generations.
- Species can interact with each other directly or indirectly.
	- Examples of indirect interaction: one species may take a resource and other species may have less.
	- Examples of direct interaction: one species may kill other species. one species may communicate with another.

Species can work together (cooperate )or compete.
- Cooperation and competition adds new selective pressures only possible for species which co-evolve across multiple generations.

# Intraspecific vs Interspecific Evolution
Intraspecific evolution refers to evolutionary changes which are caused by intraspecific variation.
- Intraspecific variation is differences in genotypes amongst individuals of the same species.
- Intraspecific evolution occurs when intraspecific competition favours individuals with specific traits: causing specialisation, diversification, and accumulation of adaptive traits over generations.


Interspecific evolution refers to evolutionary changes which are caused by interactions between different species.
- Interactions which drive interspecific evolution are...
	1) Cooperation.
	2) Competition.
	3) Predation.
	4) Hybridisation. 
- Interspecific competition drives evolutionary changes in a given species which lead to traits that reduce interspecific competition.
	- A species with less interspecific competition will compete with other species more.

Intraspecific selection builds complex traits and behaviours in species over time.

Interspecific selection acts as a filter which causes the extinction, or adaption, of less competitive species.
