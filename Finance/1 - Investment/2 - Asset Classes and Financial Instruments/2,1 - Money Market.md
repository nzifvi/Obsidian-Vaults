## Money Market
The money market is a sub-type of the fixed-income market.
- Money market mainly consists of short-term debt securities which are highly marketable.

Many of the the securities part of the money market are sold in high volume which means, to the individual, they are inaccessible.
- Money market funds usually bunch groups of investments from individual investors and form a mutual fund pool.
- Mutual fund pools are the resources of many individual investors and are then used, by the money market funds, to purchase money market securities on their behalf.
