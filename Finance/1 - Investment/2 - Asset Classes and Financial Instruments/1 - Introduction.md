## Introduction
Each broad asset class contains many specific types of securities.
- The properties of these types of securities can differ whilst remaining within the same asset class.

Financial markets are traditionally separated into 2 types of markets:
1) Money markets.
2) Capital markets.
- Each type of market has their own financial instruments with shared properties.

Financial instruments part of a money market include short-term, marketable, liquid, low-risk debt securities.
- Money market instruments are sometimes called cash equivalents or just cash.

Capital markets include more long-term and riskier financial instruments.
- Financial instruments in the capital market are a lot more diverse.
- The Capital market can be further differentiated into 4 sub-types:
	1) Longer-term bond markets.
	2) Equity markets.
	3) Derivative markets.
