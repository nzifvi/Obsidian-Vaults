## What is an Investment?
An investment is a commitment, made at a point in time, of money/another resource with the expectation of receiving future benefits from said investment.
- The resource could be money, time, etc.
- The reward is dependent upon what is being invested into (could be knowledge, money, etc).

ALL types of investment share 2 details:
1) Some resource is sacrificed at a point of time.
2) The resource is sacrificed with the expectation to receive a benefit in the future.

## Real Assets vs. [[1,2 - Financial Assets]]
Material wealth of a society ultimately determined by the productive capacity of it's economy.
- The goods and services the member of society can make.

Productive capacity of an economy is a FUNCTION of real assets of said economy: land, building, machines, knowledge.
- These real assets are used in the production of goods and services.

Real assets are land, machines, building, and knowledge.

Financial assets are stocks and bonds. They do not directly contribute to the productive capacity of a society's economy.
- Financial assets are instead a way for members of society to hold a share of the real assets.
- Mr. x invests into abc company. He then has a share in the real assets of abc company.
- When invested into, a company utilise the money from investment to purchase real assets.
- Investor's return on financial assets come from income produced by the real assets that were financed by the financial assets previously invested into by the investor.
