## Consumption Timing
Sometimes, in an economy, individuals are earning more than they wish to spend.
- Somebody in a budget surplus, for example.

Others spend more than they are able to earn.
- Retirees, for example.

It is possible to shift purchasing power from a positive net income period to a negative net income period of life.
- An amount of a positive net income can be stored in financial assets.
- In negative net income periods, the previously purchased financial assets can be sold to balance the negative net income to satisfy consumption needs.

This method allows for consumption to be shifted over the course of your life.
- This allows consumption to be allocated to periods in life which provide the greatest satisfaction.
- The timing of consumption is changed as an investment is made in the current period, which is then spent at a later point in time.
