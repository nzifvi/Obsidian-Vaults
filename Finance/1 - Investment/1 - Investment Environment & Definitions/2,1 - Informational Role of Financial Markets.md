## Informational Role of Financial Markets
Stock prices reflect investors' collective assessments of a corporation's current performance in time, as well as future prospects.
- When market is more optimistic about a corporation's current performance and future, the corporation's share price will increase.
- Greater share price means that corporation has a greater ability to raise capital, which encourages the corporation to invest.
- Thus, stock prices are a major part in the allocation of capital in market economies.

Capital markets do not always channel resources the most efficient way.
- Corporations, or entire sectors, can become "hot" for a period of time.
	- Attract a large flow of investor capital and then fail after a few years, wasting the investor capital.
- The measurement of efficiency must be careful, as nobody knows with 100% certainty which corporations will succeed and which will fail.
- It is unreasonable to...
	1) Except the market to never make mistakes.
	2) Except investors to never make mistakes.
