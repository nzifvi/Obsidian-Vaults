## Investment Portfolio
A portfolio refers to a collection of investment assets.

 Once a portfolio is established, it is updated or rebalanced by selling existing securities OR using the proceeds of the portfolio, or additional funds, to buy new securities.

## Investment Assets
Investment Assets can be differentiated into broad asset sets:
- Stocks.
- Bonds.
- Real estate.
- Commodities.
... (more exist).

Investors make two types of decisions when constructing an investment portfolio.
1) Asset Allocation.
	- Decision in what investment assets should be part of the investment portfolio.
2) Security Selection.
	- Decisions in which specific types of securities, from the given investment asset types, to hold in EACH asset class.

### Asset Allocation
Asset allocation is the decision in what investment assets should be part of the investment portfolio.

It also includes the decision of how much of a portfolio should be placed in safe assets vs. risky assets.
- Examples of safe assets: bank accounts, money market securities.
- Do not get confused between saving and safe investing.
	- Saving = not all of a positive net income is spent. Some is allocated to a portfolio which can be invested in safe or risky assets.
- A choice is made to invest savings into safe assets, risky assets, or a combination of both.

## Top-down Portfolio Construction
Top-down portfolio construction starts with asset allocation.
- An amount of money is broken into multiple parts and decided what fraction of it will be allocated to each asset type.
- This establishes the first features of the portfolio.

A top-down portfolio construction method performs analysis on the investment assets chosen BEFORE security selection.
- Modelling, [[3,2 - Security Analysis]], etc.

## Bottom-up Portfolio Construction
In this approach, securities that seem well priced are bought, without much concern for the resulting asset allocation of the portfolio.
- May result in unintended investment in a specific corporation or entire sector of industry.
- However, a bottom-up approach does focus the portfolio towards investment assets which offer good investment opportunities.
