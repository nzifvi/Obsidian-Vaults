## What are Financial Assets?
Financial assets are stocks and bonds. They do not directly contribute to the productive capacity of a society's economy.
- Financial assets are instead a way for members of society to hold a share of the real assets.
- Mr. x invests into abc company. He then has a share in the real assets of abc company.
- When invested into, a company utilise the money from investment to purchase real assets.
- Investor's return on financial assets come from income produced by the real assets that were financed by the financial assets previously invested into by the investor.

### What is a Security?
A security is a financial instrument which represents some type of value.

Securities can be bought, sold, and traded.

Securities give the holder of said securities a claim on an asset.
- Ownership in a corporation.
- Promise of repayment.


## Types of Financial Assets
Financial assets can be differentiated into three broad subsets: fixed income, equity, and derivatives.

### Fixed-income
Fixed-income, aka debt securities, promise either...
1) A fixed stream of income.
2) Stream of income determined by a specified formula.

Example: a corporate bond typically promises that the bondholder receives a fixed amount of interest each year.
- floating rate bonds promise payment depending on current interest rates.
- Two types of bonds exist: fixed rate and floating rate bonds.

Fixed-income securities refer to many types of MATURITIES and PAYMENT PROVISIONS
- Maturities = period of time investor is repaid the investment amount of the bond.
- Payment provisions = how and when interest and principal are paid to the investor.
### Equity
Equity, or common stock, represents an amount of ownership share in a corporation.

Equity holders are not promised any particular form of payment provision.
- They receive ANY dividends the corporation may pay to equity holders.
- Have an allocated ownership in the real assets of the corporation, proportional with the amount of equity they have.

Performance of equity investments is proportional with the success of the corporation.
- If corporation is less successful, equity becomes less valued.
- If corporation is more successful, equity becomes more valued.
- Due to this, equity investments tend to be riskier than fixed-income investments.

### Derivatives
Derivative securities, such as options and futures contracts, provide payoffs that are determined by the prices of OTHER assets, such as bond or stock prices.

Example: a call option, on a share of Intel stock, might turn out to be worthless if Intel's cost per share price remains below a threshold, aka exercise, price such as 20 units per share.

Derivative securities are named so because their values are DERIVED from the value of other assets.

Derivatives have become a fundamental part of the investment environment.
- Used to hedge risks (protect against financial loses).
- Transfer risk to other party.
