# Notes
Advances in Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) have inspired innovations in environment navigation for autonomous robots.
- Past methods are being retrofitted to harness abilities of ANNs.
- New, pioneering methods are not as common.
- ANN based environment mapping and navigation is computationally expensive. Furthermore, it becomes increasingly expensive the more sensors are used: driving the need to use the least amount of sensors required.

There have not been many studies regarding the implementation and use of omni-directional views, such as ones produced by LiDAR sensors, for autonomous robots.

Further studies upon in-motion robots are required to increase their ability to map environments whilst decreasing the processing demands to achieve such a task.
